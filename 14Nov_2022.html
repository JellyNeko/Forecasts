1. Monday: Anime Screening

6:30pm @ SAF G34
Theme: Japanese Folklore!
  • Mushishi follows Ginko, someone who can see and interact with 'mushi', magical creatures that appear everywhere. Follow him as he helps people afflicted by them and does research into them.
  • Natsume's Book of Friends is named after the book in the show, which has the ability to call upon the names of youkai (spirits) its user has defeated. Natsume Takashi, an orphan, winds up in possession of this book made by his grandmother, and must protect it from scheming youkai.
  • The Eccentric Family follows Yasaburou Shimogamo, the son of an influential tanuki family, whose father was killed by a group of humans called the Friday Fellows. He must struggle to avoid his own demise as he investigates his father's death!

5. Doodle Event

We are proud to announce the return of the Doodle Event! Started during the pandemic, it's become a mainstay of the society.

Each month, we give you five topics to pick from. All you need to do is draw something related to one of those topics and submit your drawing by the given deadline! There will then be a 'doodle review' in one of the Wednesday sessions where Hetty, our Artist in Residence, will be go through the submissions and give feedback. 6. LAU Karaoke

26 November!

Our second LAU event of the year is upon us! The LAU Karaoke will be held on Saturday the 26th of November. As with previous years, you can expect it to start sometime early afternoon and go well into the night.

Come join students from all over London in singing your hearts out! We're not great at singing, but we do it from the heart and that's what matters.

We start off with the usual suspects - Unravel from Tokyo Ghoul, THE HERO from One Punch Man - but somehow, we always end up singing Let It Go In 25 Languages by the end of it.

ICAS membership is required to join this event.
